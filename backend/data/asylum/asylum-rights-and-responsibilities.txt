Asylum applicants' rights and responsibilities

------------------------------------

Your rights

As an asylum applicant in the United Kingdom, you have the right to:

- be treated fairly and lawfully regardless of race, gender, age, religion, sexual orientation or disability
- practise your religion and respect people of other faiths
- have your application considered fairly and accurately
- access support and accommodation if you meet eligibility requirements
- access free healthcare from the National Health Service (NHS)
- have legal representation (free legal help may be available depending on income and case circumstances)

Various types of support may be available depending on your personal situation.

------------------------------------

Employment restrictions

You will not normally be allowed to work while your asylum application is being considered.

------------------------------------

Your responsibilities

As an asylum applicant in the United Kingdom, you must:

- cooperate with the Home Office
- provide truthful information in your asylum application
- understand that providing false information is a criminal offence and may result in imprisonment and deportation
- stay in regular contact with your caseworker
- attend all required appointments
- obey UK law

------------------------------------

Responsibilities towards children

You must care for your children by:
- supervising children under 16
- ensuring children aged 5 to 16 receive full-time education

------------------------------------

If your asylum application is refused

You must leave the United Kingdom if:
- your asylum claim is refused
- any appeal you make is unsuccessful

Until a final decision is made:
- removal action will not normally be taken against you or your dependants
